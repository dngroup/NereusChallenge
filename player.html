Current scenario testbed

Several servers are deployed with a 700Kbps to 3400Kbps upload capacity.


Each server is hosting several representations.


The MS-Stream player is trying to simultaneously retrieve descriptions of the video in the given number of servers in order to merge them and display the highest possible content quality.


According to the number of descriptions retrieved for a given video segment the intrinsical segment quality could change.


When all the descriptions are collected, the highest possible video quality is displayed.


Should some descriptions be too late for content playout, playback continuity is insured yet in sub-optimal quality.

For demonstration purposes, the buffer is limited on purpose so that we can immediately observe the impact of our actions. Therefore, due to the variable bitrate of the videos, it might happen that the player drops some segments even if its download bandwidth appears to be ok. In a real context, with a standard behaviour of the buffer of the video, the impact of the variable bitrate would not be seen.
